01. About

I'm a system software engineer at Naver Labs, where physical machines meet software. For the last few years I've owned the vehicle-side software of an autonomous shuttle and led the platform of a street-view mapping vehicle — device drivers, in-vehicle networks, custom Yocto OS images with read-only rootfs and A/B redundancy, release engineering, and the on-vehicle debugging that makes a prototype survive real service.

Before that, I worked on automotive SoC system software at Samsung Electronics (DS Division), and earned an M.S. in Computer Science researching real-time scheduling — profiling autonomous-driving workloads on embedded boards and turning the fixes into a method anyone can apply with stock Linux tools.

I care about two things: measuring before optimizing, and finishing systems to the level where they quietly just work. Lately I'm deep in PREEMPT_RT and real-time Linux, and I lean hard on AI tools to move faster from spec to verified system.

Real-Time Optimization of Autonomous Driving Workloads

M.S. thesis. Profiled an Autoware stack on a Jetson TX2 with perf/kernelshark, modeled it as a DAG, and built a 3-step heuristic using only stock Linux knobs (policy, affinity, priority). Default CFS missed deadlines and crashed the run; the method met every deadline and drove.

Optimal Task Parallelization for Global EDF

Research on conditionally optimal parallelization of real-time tasks with parallelization freedom — up to 60% schedulability gains, validated on PREEMPT_RT with real autonomous-driving programs. Published at RTSS 2019 and in IEEE Transactions on Computers.
